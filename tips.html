Understanding Plastics

Recycling plastics can be complex, but it's essential given the 40kg of plastic the average UK household discards yearly.

Identify plastics by their numbers (1-7) in the triangular symbol. PET (1), HDPE (2), and PP (5) are easier to recycle, while LDPE, polystyrene, and PVC require special recycling. Items like crisp packets and cling film cannot be recycled.

Practical Tips for Plastic Recycling

  • Screw lids back on bottles and push straws into cartons to prevent loss.
  • Flatten plastic bottles to save space and prevent rolling off conveyor belts.
  • Rinse containers thoroughly to remove food or liquid residue.
  • Ensure plastic items are dry before placing them in recycling bins.
  • Mixed materials (e.g., plastic-coated coffee cups) must go in general waste.

Understanding Metals

Most metals, including aluminium, steel, and copper, are recyclable, while mercury and lead are not.

Practical Tips for Metal Recycling

  • Take larger items and electronics to specialist recycling facilities.
  • Empty and rinse items to remove any food or liquid residue.

Other Recyclable Materials

Paper, glass, cardboard, garden waste, and food waste (if your area offers a collection service) are easily recyclable.

Practical Tips for Recycling Other Materials

  • Remove glitter and badges from greeting cards.
  • Remove plastic wrapping from newspapers and cardboard boxes.
  • Remove food residue from paper and glass products.
  • Leave lids and caps on glass jars and bottles to prevent loss during sorting.

General Recycling Tips

Aside from understanding which materials can and cannot be recycled and how to prepare these items for recycling, there are some other simple actions you can take to enhance your recycling efforts and ensure they're always done correctly.

  • Keep a designated recycling bin in your kitchen, or even two, to separate metals and plastics from paper and cardboard. Make sure it's visible and labeled for easy use by everyone in the household.
  • Display a list of non-recyclable items commonly found in your home in a prominent location, such as the fridge, to raise awareness among household members. This helps ensure only recyclable materials are placed in the bins.
  • Avoid using black bin bags or any type of bag for recycling, as they're not recyclable themselves and can lead to confusion with general waste. Most household recycling collection bins can be filled directly without a bag.
  • If you're ever unsure about what can be recycled, consult the guidance provided on your local council's website for clarification.
